# Perspective and Transfer Protocol

## Contents

1. Perspective-expansion proposal
2. Critical positioning
3. Independent judgment
4. Whole-book transfer map
5. Transfer safeguards

## 1. Perspective-Expansion Proposal

After Phase 1 completes for the relevant scope, propose at most three directions without browsing. Select directions that could materially change how the reader evaluates or uses the book:

- intellectual lineage or disciplinary position;
- competing theory or alternative explanation;
- current research, criticism, or obsolescence;
- historical, cultural, class, or institutional conditions;
- a connection to another supplied book;
- a broader question about how the model changes observation of society or self.

For each direction, state in one sentence what question it would answer and why it matters. End with one selection question. A proposal is not permission to browse; the user's selection or explicit research request is.

## 2. Critical Positioning

Use external evidence only after authorization. For the selected direction:

1. Preserve the author's source-grounded position and locator.
2. Identify the field-level question rather than searching only for the book title.
3. Prefer primary research, authoritative scholarship, or the original competing theory.
4. Distinguish factual disagreement, explanatory disagreement, and value conflict.
5. Compare definitions, evidence design, population, historical context, and uncertainty.
6. Explain what the outside view reveals and what it still cannot decide.

Use:

```text
【原书内容】作者的模型与定位
【书外补充】外部 theory, evidence, or historical context with citation
【综合归纳】relationship: support, conflict, complement, different level, or unresolved
适用范围：the conclusion's actual boundary
```

Do not manufacture an intellectual lineage, mainstream consensus, or opposition merely from thematic similarity.

## 3. Independent Judgment

Build the reader's position with headings rather than a new evidence label:

- `我的判断`: what the reader currently accepts, rejects, or suspends;
- `依据`: source arguments and verified outside evidence supporting that stance;
- `保留意见`: unresolved evidence, value choices, or competing explanations;
- `适用边界`: conditions under which the judgment should change or fail.

Separate agreement with evidence from agreement with the author's values. Name the book's most explanatory idea, most vulnerable premise, and most important unasked question when supported.

## 4. Whole-Book Transfer Map

Assess every domain below. Mark a domain `值得深入`, `有限相关`, or `不宜迁移`, with one reason. Expand only `值得深入` domains.

### Writing Topics and Arguments

- observation or tension worth writing about;
- question and provisional thesis;
- theoretical chain supporting the thesis;
- strongest counterargument or alternative explanation;
- evidence still needed before publication.

### Daily Observation

- observable situation or recurring signal;
- distinction the theory helps the reader notice;
- competing interpretation to keep visible;
- condition that would falsify or weaken the observation.

### Work and Relationships

- situation in which the model may clarify roles, incentives, communication, or decisions;
- judgment steps before acting;
- contextual or power conditions;
- misuse boundary, especially stereotyping, mind-reading, or amateur diagnosis.

### Self-Reflection

- behavior pattern framed as a hypothesis, not a diagnosis;
- evidence and counterevidence to observe;
- one small reversible experiment or reflection prompt;
- condition for revising the self-explanation.

### Connections with Other Books

- connect only to a supplied book or externally verified source;
- classify the relationship as agreement, conflict, complement, different level, or different question;
- compare concepts and assumptions rather than titles or themes alone;
- when evidence is absent, describe the kind of book or theory needed and mark the specific connection `【无法确认】`.

## 5. Transfer Safeguards

- Do not present transfer as proof that the theory is true.
- Do not apply group tendencies directly to an individual.
- Do not turn one anecdote into a general law.
- Do not replace professional medical, legal, or financial guidance with a book model; verify current standards in Phase 2 first.
- Prefer observable criteria, reversible experiments, and explicit failure conditions over prescriptions.
- For partial chapters, excerpts, or notes, allow only a provisional transfer tied to that scope; never output a complete whole-book transfer map.
